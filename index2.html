CRAO — Central Retinal Artery Occlusion
EMERGENCY ▾
Triggers
Sudden painless vision loss APD Amaurosis fugax Cherry red spot
⚠ Stroke equivalent. Time-sensitive. Refer immediately to ED. Activate TIA/CVA pathway.
Immediate Workup
  • CBC w/diff, PT/PTT, ESR, CRP (r/o GCA if >=55 yo)
  • Carotid Doppler, echo (cardiac embolic source)
  • MRI/MRA brain
  • Temporal artery biopsy within 1 week if GCA suspected
GCA Protocol (>=55 yo with jaw claudication / scalp tenderness)
  • Start steroids IMMEDIATELY — do not wait for biopsy
  • If vision loss: IV methylprednisolone 1g/day x3d → oral prednisone 1 mg/kg/day
  • If no vision loss: Prednisone 40–60 mg/day PO

Acute Angle-Closure Glaucoma
EMERGENCY ▾
Triggers
IOP >40 mmHg Nausea / vomiting Headache Halos around lights Fixed mid-dilated pupil
IOP-Lowering Protocol (initiate immediately)
  • Brimonidine 0.2% — 1 gtt
  • Dorzolamide-Timolol (Cosopt) — 1 gtt q15 min x2
  • Acetazolamide (Diamox) — 2 x 250 mg PO now
  • Refer on-call OMD immediately after initiation
Post-op cataract: if within 1 week → refer to surgeon's team, not ED.

Globe Rupture / Chemical Burn / Orbital Fracture
EMERGENCY ▾
⚠ Chemical burn: IRRIGATE FIRST. Do not delay irrigation for H&P or vitals.
Chemical Burn Protocol
  • Irrigate immediately (NS or tap water), continue until pH 7.0–7.4
  • Sweep fornices for residual particulate
  • Fluorescein staining (Seidel test) after irrigation
  • Refer OD/OMD urgently same day
Globe Rupture / Penetrating FB
  • Do NOT patch (pressure on globe); use eye shield only
  • Fluorescein + Seidel sign (streaming = positive)
  • Keflex 250–500 mg PO q6h x5-7d (laceration present)
  • Refer on-call OMD emergently

Corneal Ulcer — 3-2-1 Rule
URGENT ▾
Refer to OMD if any present
  • >3 mm from visual axis
  • 2 or more lesions
  • 1 mm or closer to visual axis
FM Initial Management
  • Culture before starting antibiotics
  • 4th-gen fluoroquinolone: Moxifloxacin or Ciprofloxacin
  • D/C steroids, contact lenses, cosmetics
  • No patch (traps bacteria)

Anterior Uveitis (Iritis)
URGENT ▾
Triggers
Photophobia Ciliary flush (limbal injection) AC cells + flare Dull ache
Workup (50% idiopathic — test by suspicion)
  • HLA-B27, ANA, CBC, ESR, CRP
  • RPR / FTA-ABS (syphilis), Lyme, ACE (sarcoid)
  • Chest XR if sarcoid suspected
FM Initial Tx
  • Prednisolone acetate 1% q2h (taper per OD)
  • Cyclopentolate 1% BID-TID (prevents synechiae)
  • OD follow-up 24–72h

GCA Systemic Steroid Dosing
GCA Protocol ▾
  • Vision loss present: IV methylprednisolone 1g/day x3d → prednisone 1 mg/kg/day
  • No vision loss: Prednisone 40–60 mg/day PO
  • Do NOT wait for biopsy
  • Biopsy within 1 week (steroids won't negate ~2 weeks)
  • Slow taper over months; monitor ESR/CRP
Start PPI + Calcium/VitD + bone density planning at initiation for long-course treatment.
When to Refer: OD vs OMD vs ED
Referral Guide ▾
On-Call OD — Same Day Urgent
  • Corneal ulcer (not meeting 3-2-1)
  • Corneal abrasion (follow-up 24-48h)
  • Anterior uveitis (24-72h)
  • New flashes + floaters (no curtain)
On-Call OMD — Emergent
  • Globe rupture / penetrating FB
  • Endophthalmitis
  • Acute angle closure (after IOP-lowering started)
  • Orbital cellulitis with fever
  • Retinal detachment confirmed
  • Corneal ulcer meeting 3-2-1
Emergency Department
  • CRAO — activate stroke pathway
  • CN III palsy pupil-involved — MRA brain
  • GCA with vision loss — IV steroids
  • Chemical burn (irrigate first, then ED or OD)
When in doubt: call on-call OD first. They triage to OMD or ED faster than guessing.
FM Ocular Exam Checklist
Exam ▾
  • Visual Acuity — Snellen each eye; corrected/uncorrected
  • Pupils — Direct/consensual; swinging flashlight for RAPD
  • EOMs — H-pattern; pain, limitation, diplopia
  • Confrontation VF — Each eye separately
  • IOP — Tonopen; normal 10–21 mmHg
  • Anterior Segment — Penlight/slit lamp: lids, conj, cornea, AC
  • Fluorescein — Cobalt blue; uptake = epithelial defect
  • Fundus — Dilated/direct: disc, macula, vessels
RAPD = CN II compromise or severe retinal disease on affected side. Always do swinging flashlight if vision complaint.
Red Eye — Cannot-Miss Red Flags
Red Flags ▾
Any of these → NOT simple conjunctivitis. Refer same day.
  • Vision loss or change
  • Moderate–severe pain (vs. mild gritty discomfort)
  • Photophobia
  • Proptosis
  • RAPD (afferent pupillary defect)
  • Anisocoria
  • Corneal opacity or fluorescein uptake
  • Hyphema or hypopyon
  • Mucopurulent discharge + CL use
  • Recent intraocular surgery
  • Penetrating trauma history
